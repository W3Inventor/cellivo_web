# Cellivo SEO Audit

Date: April 17, 2026

## Scope And Method

- Audited the current local SSR build running from this repo at `http://127.0.0.1:8080`.
- Crawled 35 routes, including the homepage, all feature pages, `/mobile-shop-pos-system`, `/pos-system-for-phone-shop`, `/about`, `/contact`, `/pricing`, `/blog`, all blog posts, legal pages, and `noindex` auth pages.
- Checked rendered HTML, titles, descriptions, canonicals, robots, H1 usage, schema, internal/external links, sitemap/robots, redirects, orphaning, and mobile lab metrics.
- Ran mobile lab checks in headless Chromium on `/`, `/mobile-shop-pos-system`, `/pos-system-for-phone-shop`, `/features`, `/about`, `/contact`, `/pricing`, and `/blog`.
- Important assumption: this audit covers the site in this codebase. The live `https://cellivo.com` domain currently responds as a different WordPress property, so canonical/deployment-domain alignment must be validated before launch.

## Summary Score

Overall SEO score: **72 / 100**

What is working well:

- No missing titles.
- No duplicate titles.
- No missing meta descriptions.
- No duplicate meta descriptions.
- Every audited page has exactly one H1.
- No missing `img` alt text was found in rendered HTML.
- 404 handling is correct: HTTP 404 plus `noindex, nofollow`.
- SSR output is crawlable and canonicals are present on all audited pages.
- The two priority landing pages are strongly linked internally.

Main reasons the score is not higher:

- Two feature pages are true orphans.
- One blog post is missing from the sitemap.
- Keyword cannibalization risk is high across the main commercial landing pages.
- Local SEO trust signals are weak despite Sri Lanka targeting.
- Schema is inconsistent across feature pages.
- First-load JavaScript is heavy.
- Mobile tap targets and some mobile text sizes are below recommended sizes.
- Several important pages are thin or underdeveloped for their target intent.

### Weak Areas

- The URL structure is clean and SEO-friendly, but the hub-and-spoke implementation is incomplete.
- `/features` is the logical hub, yet it does not expose every feature page.
- Navigation and footer emphasize only a subset of feature pages, which compresses authority into a few URLs and leaves others weak.
- Blog articles mostly rely on nav/footer/CTA links instead of contextual in-article links to solution pages.
- Home, `/mobile-shop-pos-system`, `/pos-system-for-phone-shop`, `/pos-for-mobile-shops`, and `/pos-system-sri-lanka` are all trying to rank in a closely related commercial cluster without a strict hierarchy.

### Recommended Structure Improvements

- Make `/mobile-shop-pos-system` the main broad “platform” pillar or keep that role on `/`; do not let both pages compete for the same primary query.
- Keep `/pos-system-for-phone-shop` as the sales/billing-intent landing page.
- Keep `/pos-system-sri-lanka` as the local-intent landing page.
- Either expand `/pos-for-mobile-shops` into a genuinely different intent page or consolidate it into `/mobile-shop-pos-system`.
- Promote all strategic feature pages through `/features`, not just a subset.
- Add contextual spokes from blog content into the most relevant feature pages.

### Performance Issues

- The shared first-load JavaScript bundle is large: `1038.8KB` raw, `262.1KB` gzipped.
- Shared CSS is reasonable: `81.3KB` raw, `14.2KB` gzipped.
- First-load payload on the homepage was about `1241.8KB`, dominated by JavaScript.
- CLS is healthy in local testing (`0` on tested pages), so stability is not the current bottleneck.
- LCP is acceptable in local SSR conditions, but the bundle size can hurt real-device performance on slower Sri Lankan mobile connections.
- Image payload is not the main issue here; no major image bloat was detected on the tested routes.

### Suggested Fixes

- Split the large shared route bundle so marketing routes do not all ship in one monolithic JS file.
- Defer or lazily load non-critical below-the-fold sections and heavy motion code.
- Ensure Brotli/Gzip is enabled on the production host or CDN.
- Keep LCP text-first and avoid turning hero sections into image-heavy blocks.
- Raise mobile tap target sizes and minimum text sizes, because perceived usability and engagement affect SEO outcomes indirectly.

## 8. Content And SEO Strategy Audit

### Keyword Targeting And Cannibalization

Highest-risk overlap cluster:

- `/`
- `/mobile-shop-pos-system`
- `/pos-system-for-phone-shop`
- `/pos-for-mobile-shops`
- `/pos-system-sri-lanka`
- `/features`

Why the risk is high:

- Titles and H1s sit in the same “phone shop POS / mobile shop POS” cluster.
- The two main landing pages get the bulk of exact-match anchors.
- `/pos-for-mobile-shops` is thin and not clearly differentiated from `/mobile-shop-pos-system`.
- `/features` and `/` also use platform-level messaging that competes with the deeper landing pages.

### Keyword Placement Gaps

Pages where the first paragraph is too generic and does not strongly reinforce the page keyword:

- `/billing-software-for-mobile-shop`
- `/features`
- `/imei-tracking-pos-system`
- `/integrations`
- `/inventory-management-system`
- `/settings-security-integrations`
- `/pricing`

### Content Gaps

- The blog exists, but it is still a small cluster with only 6 short posts.
- Missing clear comparison/decision content, such as generic POS vs phone-shop POS, pricing guidance, migration/offboarding, staff training, case studies, and local Sri Lanka buying guides.
- No deep content cluster exists for accessories, spare parts, trade-ins, or wholesale/B2B phone-shop workflows.
- The local content cluster is only lightly built out beyond `/pos-system-sri-lanka` and `/contact`.

### Strategic Recommendations

- Assign one primary keyword and one supporting-intent role to each commercial landing page.
- Expand or consolidate overlapping pages instead of letting multiple URLs chase nearly identical intent.
- Grow the blog into supporting topic clusters that feed the commercial pages with contextual links.

## 9. Local SEO Audit

### Current Local Strength

- `/pos-system-sri-lanka` is a useful local landing page.
- `/contact` mentions Sri Lanka and includes `areaServed: Sri Lanka`.
- `/about` and `/contact` mention W3Inventor as a Sri Lanka-based company.

### Missing Local Elements

- No real Sri Lankan phone number is used; the site uses a placeholder US-style number.
- No real business address is present in the footer or contact schema.
- No city-level or branch-level proof is present beyond copy mentions.
- `LocalBusiness` schema is incomplete.
- Footer address text is generic and does not support trust or map-based local relevance.

### Local SEO Verdict

Local SEO intent is present in copy, but local trust signals are still weak. This site is not yet sending strong enough real-world business signals for competitive Sri Lanka queries.

1. `/reload-topup-services` and `/subscription-billing-support` are orphan pages.
2. `/blog/customer-loyalty-programs-phone-shops` is missing from the sitemap.
3. The main commercial landing pages are competing for the same keyword cluster.
4. `/pricing` CTA buttons are not crawlable links.
5. Local SEO trust signals are weak because contact data is placeholder and schema is incomplete.
6. The shared first-load JS bundle is too large for a marketing site.
7. Mobile tap targets and some text sizes are too small across key pages.
8. Most feature pages lack `SoftwareApplication` schema.
9. Several high-value pages are thin or underdeveloped, especially `/pos-for-mobile-shops`, `/pos-system-sri-lanka`, and `/pricing`.
10. Meta descriptions and some titles are longer than ideal on important pages.

### Quick Wins

- Add `/blog/customer-loyalty-programs-phone-shops` to the sitemap.
- Add internal links to `/reload-topup-services` and `/subscription-billing-support`.
- Turn `/pricing` CTAs into real links to `/signup` and `/contact`.
- Replace placeholder NAP details with real Sri Lankan business information.
- Shorten overlong meta descriptions on the biggest landing pages.
- Add `SoftwareApplication` schema to the high-intent feature pages missing it.

### Long-Term Improvements

- Consolidate or clearly differentiate overlapping commercial landing pages.
- Expand the blog into deeper intent-based topic clusters with contextual linking.
- Build stronger local SEO assets around real business data and Sri Lankan proof.
- Split the monolithic JS bundle to improve first-load performance.
- Strengthen the `/features` hub so it actually represents the full feature set.

### Priority Action Plan

1. Fix indexation/discovery issues first: sitemap omission, orphan pages, and pricing CTA links.

4. Expand thin commercial pages and strengthen keyword placement in the first paragraph.
5. Add missing commercial schema to feature and pricing pages.
6. Improve supporting-page internal links from the features hub and blog content.
7. Reduce first-load JS and increase mobile tap target/text sizes.
